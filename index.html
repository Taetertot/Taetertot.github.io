About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Washington Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering, specializing in Health Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). I'm proud to be a member of DUB and co-advised by Dr. James Fogarty and Dr. Sean Munson in the Intentional Traces Collective Lab of Human Centered Design & Engineering (HCDE) department.

My academic journey includes a BBA in Information Systems with a minor in Applied Statistics, and an MS in Healthcare Management & Informatics from Kennesaw State University, where I was advised by Dr. Adriane Randolph in The BrainLab.

What drives me is the belief that technology should be a thoughtful partner in health management, not just a data collector. I'm particularly interested in how we can design systems that truly understand and adapt to the complex realities of living with multiple health conditions.

Bridging Technology & Health

PhD Candidate in Health Human-Computer Interaction

"Leveraging wearable technology to support behavioral change in mental and physical health"

Research Interests

User & Participant Engagement Behavioral Change Support Health Wearables & Tracking Mental-Physical Comorbidities Health Technology & Informatics Healthcare Technology Integration

My research focus centers on leveraging health tracking and wearable applications for behavioral change support in comorbid and mental health populations. I'm passionate about understanding how technology can meaningfully support people's health journeys, particularly those managing complex, interconnected health conditions.
